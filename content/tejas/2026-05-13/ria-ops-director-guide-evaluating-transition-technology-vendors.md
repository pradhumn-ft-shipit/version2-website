Every vendor in the advisor transitions space says they automate the process. But "automate" means different things depending on whether you're a document management company that bolted transitions on as a feature, or a platform built from day one to handle the full workflow end-to-end. Here's how to tell the difference — and how to ask the right questions before you sign.

## Why This Decision Has Direct AUM Impact

This isn't an IT decision. It's a revenue decision.

The data makes it uncomfortable: 82% of advisors report losing prospects, and 67% report losing clients, due to subpar technology during onboarding. The platform your RIA chooses for transitions affects your recruiting pitch, your ops team's capacity, and your FINRA exam readiness.

When an advisor moves to your firm, every day the transition takes is another day their former employer is watching for clients who hesitate. The technology you choose either compresses that window or extends it. There's no neutral.

## The 7 Non-Negotiables for Transition Technology in 2026

Before you evaluate pricing, features, or aesthetics, run every vendor through these seven filters. A platform that fails any one of them creates risk for your firm.

**1. Pre-submission validation against each custodian's specific requirements — not generic**

Fidelity's form requirements differ from Schwab's, which differ from Pershing's. A platform that runs a generic "form check" will still generate custodian-specific NIGOs at submission time. You need pre-validation that knows each custodian's current rules and catches errors before the form leaves your office.

**2. Multi-custodian support — both ACATS and non-ACATS**

Advisors moving to your firm often hold accounts across multiple custodians. If your transition platform handles Schwab but requires manual workarounds for Pershing, you're still doing manual work. Ask specifically which custodians are supported and what that support actually means in practice.

**3. Automated audit trail that exports for FINRA/SEC exams**

Regulators want to see who touched what, when, and how errors were caught. FINRA's 2026 Annual Regulatory Oversight Report flagged recordkeeping deficiencies more than 50 times. "We have logs" is not the same as "here is a clean, timestamped, exportable audit trail for Account X from day one to transfer complete." Make sure the vendor can show you the latter.

**4. Real-time status dashboard with exception alerts**

Your ops team should not be learning about delayed transfers by calling advisors. You need a dashboard that shows every transition by status — pending signatures, submitted, in review, rejected, completed — with proactive alerts when a transition is approaching risk (deadline approaching, client hasn't signed).

**5. CRM integration with your current stack — proven in a live demo, not a slide**

Ask them to show you the integration live. "We integrate with Salesforce" can mean anything from a full bidirectional sync to a CSV import. If they can't demo the integration with your actual CRM during the sales process, it's not ready.

**6. Contract assignability in an M&A event**

RIA M&A activity is accelerating in 2026. If your firm is acquired, or acquires another firm, will your transition platform contract transfer? A platform that locks you into a non-assignable agreement creates risk during precisely the moment you need operational continuity most. Read the contract before you sign it.

**7. Data ownership clarity post-termination**

What happens to your client data if you leave the platform? Who owns it, how do you extract it, in what format, and on what timeline? This is a data sovereignty question with direct compliance implications under Regulation S-P.

## 12 Questions to Ask Every Vendor in a Demo

Print these out. Ask them in order. The vendor's answer to question one will tell you more about their platform's actual capabilities than any slide deck.

1. Show me a NIGO occurring — what happens to the workflow, who is notified, and how is it resolved?
2. Which custodians do you support, and how does your platform handle each custodian's specific field validation rules?
3. Can you show me a complete, exportable audit trail for a finished transition?
4. What is your average client NIGO rate across your customer base?
5. Show me a live integration with [your CRM] — not slides, a live connection.
6. What happens to our client data if we terminate the contract?
7. Is the contract assignable in an M&A event?
8. What is your SOC 2 Type II certification status?
9. Walk me through a non-ACATS transfer — what does the workflow look like?
10. What is your typical implementation timeline from contract to first live transition?
11. Show me the real-time status dashboard and how exception alerts are triggered.
12. What is your data encryption standard for client PII in transit and at rest?

A vendor who cannot answer questions 1, 3, and 11 with live demonstrations — not screenshots, live demos — does not have the product they're describing.

## How to Score Vendors Against Each Other

Build a simple scoring matrix. Weight the seven non-negotiables as pass/fail. Then score the 12-question responses on a 1–3 scale. Add a column for any auto-disqualifying red flags.

**Auto-disqualifiers:**
- Cannot demonstrate NIGO prevention workflow live
- No exportable audit trail
- Vague custodian coverage ("we support most major custodians")
- No SOC 2 certification
- Contract is not assignable in M&A events

Treat price as the last factor, not the first. A $10/day cost difference between platforms is irrelevant if a 20% NIGO rate costs you $50,000 per transition in ops rework, extended timelines, and client attrition risk.

## Why Purpose-Built Outperforms Swiss-Army-Knife

Most platforms in this space were built for a different workflow and adapted for advisor transitions. Document management companies that added a transitions module. CRM platforms that built a repapering feature. Salesforce-native solutions that require your team to use Salesforce the way the platform needs it.

Purpose-built transition platforms are different. The architecture starts from the transition workflow — custodian-specific validation, multi-step NIGO prevention, advisor and client communication triggers — rather than retrofitting transition logic onto a document or CRM foundation.

The difference shows up in the numbers. Purpose-built transition automation delivers 75% faster end-to-end transitions, 95% fewer NIGOs, and 90% reduction in manual work. These aren't feature claims. They're the natural outcome of building the product around the problem rather than adapting a different product to it.

Before your next vendor demo, print the 12 questions above. Ask them in order. Take notes. The platform that can answer every question live — without redirecting to slides, "checking with the team," or promising a future roadmap feature — is the platform that's actually ready to run your transitions.

The problem isn't people. It's outdated transition processes. And the decision you make about transition technology is the decision that either fixes that problem or perpetuates it.
