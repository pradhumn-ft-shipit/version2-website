# Merrill Lynch Breakaway: The 2026 FINRA Compliance Checklist for Going Independent

A Merrill Lynch advisor going independent in 2026 needs to clear three distinct compliance lanes simultaneously: FINRA registration and Form U5 handling, Broker Protocol versus non-protocol obligations, and Merrill's specific contractual and garden-leave provisions. Get any one of those wrong and you are looking at TRO exposure, U5 amendments that follow you for years, and client-data restrictions that delay your launch. This checklist walks through every item a Merrill breakaway needs to clear before resignation, on resignation day, and during the first 30 days at your new firm.

## Merrill Lynch and the Broker Protocol: The 2026 Position

Merrill Lynch withdrew from the Protocol for Broker Recruiting in November 2017. As of 2026, that withdrawal is still in force. Practically, that means a Merrill advisor leaving for an independent or RIA channel cannot rely on the Protocol's safe harbor for taking client name, address, phone, email, and account-title information. Every Merrill breakaway in 2026 is a non-protocol exit and must be planned accordingly.

The non-protocol reality changes everything about the resignation playbook. You cannot prepare client contact spreadsheets before resignation. You cannot take Merrill-issued account statements or trade confirmations. You cannot use Merrill-issued email lists, internal CRM exports, or printed client books. Your TRO risk profile is materially higher than a protocol-firm exit. This is the single most important pre-departure fact for any 2026 Merrill breakaway.

## Pre-Resignation Checklist (T-90 to T-1)

The pre-resignation window is where most TRO exposure is created. Everything you do in the 90 days before resignation is discoverable. Here is what you can and cannot do during this window.

**Can do:**

- Sign a transition agreement with the receiving firm (RIA or independent broker-dealer)
- Complete Form U4 and state registration paperwork through the receiving firm (held pending until resignation)
- Open a new entity (LLC or RIA), register with the SEC or state regulator, file the ADV
- Reach out to your destination custodian (Schwab, [Fidelity](https://www.fidelity.com/wealth-services/working-with-fidelity), [Pershing](https://www.pershing.com), etc.) and complete custodial onboarding paperwork
- Reconstruct client contact information from public sources (public phone directories, LinkedIn, public-record search) after resignation
- Review your Merrill employment agreement, especially the non-solicitation, non-compete, and confidentiality clauses

**Cannot do:**

- Copy, export, photograph, or otherwise extract client contact data from Merrill systems
- Forward Merrill emails to personal accounts
- Print client lists, trade blotters, or account statements
- Use Merrill resources (time, equipment, support staff) to plan or execute the transition
- Pre-solicit clients before resignation date
- Discuss the move with Merrill colleagues outside a small, NDA-bound transition team

The single highest-frequency TRO trigger in non-protocol exits is pre-resignation client communication. Wait.

## Resignation-Day Compliance Tasks

Resignation day is choreographed. The sequence matters because U5 timing, garden leave, and your first compliant client outreach are all triggered by the resignation timestamp.

| Hour | Task |
|---|---|
| 7:00–8:30 AM | Submit written resignation to manager and compliance via the channels specified in your employment agreement |
| 8:30–9:30 AM | Return Merrill-issued equipment (laptop, phone, badge); sign the equipment-return acknowledgment |
| 9:30 AM | Confirm Form U5 will be filed by Merrill within 30 days (FINRA Rule 1010 deadline) |
| 10:00 AM | Receiving firm files Form U4 amendment to make you registered through the new firm |
| 10:00 AM–EOD | Receiving firm or your new RIA files state registrations as required |
| EOD | Send first-touch tombstone announcement to clients (name, new firm, phone, email — NO solicitation language) |

The tombstone announcement is the key compliance artifact. It must be a factual notice of new affiliation, must not mention Merrill product comparisons, must not solicit account transfers, and must not be sent to a list constructed from Merrill records. A clean tombstone language template usually reads: "I have joined [New Firm]. My new contact information is [phone, email, address]. Please reach out if you would like to discuss your goals."

## The 30-Day Post-Resignation Window

The 30 days following resignation are when Merrill will file your Form U5, you will start receiving client inquiries, and you will likely receive a cease-and-desist letter if Merrill's compliance team believes you crossed any line. Manage this window deliberately.

**Form U5 monitoring.** Merrill is required to file your Form U5 within 30 days of termination. Pull a copy as soon as it posts to BrokerCheck. If the reason for termination is anything other than "voluntary," document the basis for any disagreement and prepare a Form U5 comment if needed. The comment lives on your BrokerCheck profile permanently, so the language matters.

**Garden leave.** Merrill's standard advisor contract contains a garden-leave provision restricting solicitation for a defined period (commonly 30-60 days, sometimes longer for senior advisors). Read your contract. Garden-leave violations are the most common TRO basis.

**Client outreach scripts.** Train your team on what they can and cannot say. Acceptable: "I have moved to [New Firm]. If you would like to learn more, here is how to reach me." Not acceptable: "Your accounts at Merrill are not safe, you should move." Document the script and have new hires sign acknowledgment.

**Repapering throughput.** Once a client says yes, the operational clock starts. The industry median time from client yes to account funded is 4-6 weeks. ## Documentation a Clean Exit Requires

A defensible breakaway leaves a clean paper trail. The minimum documentation set:

- Signed transition agreement with the receiving firm, dated and witnessed
- Resignation letter, time-stamped, with delivery confirmation
- Equipment return acknowledgment from Merrill
- Merrill exit-interview documentation if conducted
- All client outreach scripts and contact logs
- Copies of every tombstone announcement, dated
- Form U4 filing confirmation, Form U5 receipt
- Garden-leave compliance attestations
- Any cease-and-desist correspondence and your written response

Keep this set for seven years. If a Merrill-initiated arbitration or FINRA Enforcement matter emerges later, the paper trail is the defense.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Merrill Lynch a Protocol firm in 2026?**
No. Merrill Lynch withdrew from the Broker Protocol in November 2017 and remains a non-protocol firm in 2026. All Merrill breakaways must plan as non-protocol exits.

**Can I reconstruct my client list from public sources after resignation?**
Yes. Public-source reconstruction — LinkedIn, public directories, public-record searches — is permissible after resignation. The constraint is that you cannot use any data extracted from Merrill systems or any pre-built list you maintained while at Merrill.

**How long is Merrill's garden-leave?**
Garden-leave provisions vary by contract generation and seniority. Most current Merrill advisor contracts include a 30-60 day non-solicitation window. Senior advisors and team leads often have longer windows. Read the specific contract you signed.

**When does Merrill file my Form U5?**
FINRA Rule 1010 requires the prior firm to file Form U5 within 30 days of termination. Monitor BrokerCheck and pull a copy as soon as it posts.

**What is the biggest TRO risk in a non-protocol Merrill exit?**
Pre-resignation client communication or any extraction of client data from Merrill systems. Both are discoverable in litigation and both have produced TROs in 2024 and 2025 Merrill departures.
